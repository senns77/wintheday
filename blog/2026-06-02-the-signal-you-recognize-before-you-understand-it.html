The Signal You Recognize Before You Understand It

At twelve, I saw Tony Robbins on TV and something said 'that's who I am.' Not inspiration. Recognition. There's a difference.

← Back to the dispatch

I was twelve years old, sitting in front of the television. Tony Robbins came on. I had no idea who he was. No context. No frame of reference. Just a man on a screen talking with a kind of energy I'd never seen before.

And something inside me said: that's who I am.

Not 'I want to be like him.' Not 'maybe someday.' Present tense. Calm. Certain. The same way you might recognize your own handwriting or hear your name called in a crowded room. It wasn't admiration. It was identification. The soul recognizing its own signal before the mind had any business weighing in.

I didn't understand it then. I don't think I was supposed to. But I also didn't argue with it. I just let it sit there, quiet and true, while the rest of my life unfolded around it.

The Difference Between Wanting and Knowing

Wanting is future-facing. It lives in the gap between here and there. It's built on lack, on distance, on the assumption that you are not yet the thing you're reaching for. Wanting is the mind scanning the horizon for evidence that you might one day be enough.

Knowing is present tense. It doesn't need evidence because it isn't making an argument. It's not trying to convince you of anything. It just is. The soul doesn't speak in conditionals. It doesn't say 'if I work hard enough' or 'when the timing is right.' It says 'this is who I am' and then it waits for you to catch up.

That moment at twelve wasn't the beginning of a journey toward becoming something. It was the recognition of something that already existed. The identity was there before the evidence. Before the skills. Before the resume or the results or the external validation. The signal came first.

David Cameron Gikandi writes that consciousness precedes form. That the internal state doesn't wait for the external condition to give it permission. The version of you that already has what you're building toward isn't a future self you're trying to become. It's a present reality in consciousness that you're learning to inhabit.

The twelve-year-old version of me didn't have a plan. Didn't have a strategy. Didn't know how any of it would unfold. But the knowing was there, sourceless and steady, the same way it showed up at ten when I knew my name would be called for that Wayne Gretzky prize. The same way it shows up now when I'm building in the middle of pressure and chaos and something in me just knows the deal is coming before I have any logical reason to believe it.

Your soul has a signal. A frequency. A way of recognizing itself in the world before your mind has any business understanding why. The question isn't whether the signal is real. The question is whether you're willing to trust it before the evidence arrives.
